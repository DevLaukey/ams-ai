A defibrillator is a medical device used to deliver an electric shock to the heart in cases of life-threatening arrhythmias, particularly ventricular fibrillation. The shock helps restore normal heart rhythm. Defibrillators are crucial in emergency situations like cardiac arrest and can significantly improve survival rates when used promptly. There are different types of defibrillators, including: 1. Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs): Designed for public use, these are often found in public spaces and can be used by people with minimal training. 2. Manual Defibrillators: Used by healthcare professionals, these allow for more control over the shock delivery. 3. Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillators (ICDs): Surgically implanted devices for patients at high risk of sudden cardiac arrest.
